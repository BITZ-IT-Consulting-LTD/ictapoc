## 2. The "Technical Interrogation" (Alex Fido's Feedback)
- [ ] **The Digitization Fallacy:** Address the critique that "Digitization improves service delivery." Refocus the report on **Digitalization (Process Change)** as the real driver.
- [ ] **PKI & Digital Signatures:** Interrogate the 1-to-1 mapping fallacy. A paper signature requirement does NOT automatically mean an automated system needs PKI. Identify where a "Trusted Service" is truly mandatory.
- [ ] **Technical Readiness Audit:** Document why ICTA (est. 2013) still lacks Disaster Recovery (DR), shared services, and cloud adoption. Identify the real "blockers."

## 3. Data Governance & Legal Foundations
- [ ] **Governance: The "Elephant in the Room":** Address the institutional and policy blockers to shared services.
- [ ] **Archives & Records Act:** Align all recommendations with the **Public Archives and Documentation Act (KNAD)** and the gazetted EDRMS standards.
- [ ] **Long-Term Records Handling:** Develop a strategy for **99-year Land Leases** and other high-retention records. How are these treated in a digital environment?

## 4. Metadata & Cataloging
- [ ] **Metadata Standards:** Establish clear metadata and cataloging standards (the "Who/What/When" of the 22,000 digitized records) to ensure they are actually usable on eCitizen.
- [ ] **Silo Integration:** Prioritize breaking the silos between **Civil Registration Services (CRS)** and other consumer MDAs.

## 5. Implementation Roadmap
- [ ] **Ground in Reality:** All recommendations for Milestone 2/3 must be "implementable now" on the current architecture.
- [ ] **Prioritization Matrix:** Finalize the matrix using **Volumes, Service Criticality, and Data Value** as the primary weights.
- [ ] **Service Ownership:** Explicitly identify the "Service Owners" within the prioritized MDAs to ensure post-study sustainability.
