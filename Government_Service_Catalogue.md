## Public Service Commission

### Commission  Core and support Services
- **Service Type:** G2C (Government to Citizen)
- **Digital Maturity:** 2 - Developing
- **Delivery Channels:** In-person Web Contact Centre
- **Process Complexity:** >7 steps
- **Pain Points:** Manual forms Paper-based files Lack of system integration
- **Description:** The Public Service Commission is an independent Commission established under Article 233 of the Constitution of Kenya, 2010. Its mandate is spelt out in Articles 234, 155(3)(a), 158(2)(3) and (4), 171(2), 230(2)(b) and 236 of the Constitution, as follows:
i.	Establish and abolish of offices in the public service;
ii.	Appoint persons to hold or act in those offices, and to confirm appointments;
iii.	Exercise disciplinary control over and remove persons holding or acting in those offices;
iv.	Promote the national values and principles of governance; and values and principles of public service as referred to in Articles 10 and 232 throughout the public service;
v.	Investigate, monitor and evaluate the organization, administration and personnel practices of the public service;
vi.	Ensure that the public service is efficient and effective;
vii.	Develop human resources in the public service;
viii.	Review and make recommendations to the national government in respect of conditions of service, code of conduct and qualifications of officers in the public service;
ix.	Evaluate and report to the President and Parliament on the extent to which the values and principles mentioned in Articles 10 and 232 are complied with in the public service;
x.	Hear and determine appeals in respect of county governments’ public service;
xi.	Nominating persons to the Judicial Service Commission and Salaries Remuneration Commission under Articles 171(2) and 230(2)(b) respectively;
xii.	Recommending persons to be appointed as Principal Secretaries under Article 155(3)(a);
xiii.	Receiving petitions for the removal of the Director of Public Prosecutions and recommending appointment of a tribunal to investigate the complaints under Article 158(2)(3) and (4);
xiv.	Promotion of good governance, ethics and integrity in the public service in accordance with the Constitution and other legislations; and
xv.	Performing any other functions and exercise any other powers conferred by national legislation. This include:
i.	Administering the Declaration of Incomes, Assets and Liabilities.
ii.	Receiving and processing petitions from removal of:
•	The Registrar and Deputy Registrar of Political parties, and
•	The Chairperson and Members of the Independent Policing Oversight Authority.

The service type includes the following G2C, G2G, G2B and internal

Service status - for live (Job application system, Mail management, Electronic Document Management system), all the other services mentioned are under design
