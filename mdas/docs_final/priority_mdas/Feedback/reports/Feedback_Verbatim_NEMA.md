## Respondent: nan (NEMA)

### Which workshop day is this feedback for?
Day 2: MDA Process Feedback

### Which breakout room are you in? (Day 2 only)
Room 3: Policy, Economy & Foundational Systems

### Participant MDA / Organization
NEMA

### Which MDA presentation are you giving feedback on?
National Environment Management Authority

### A1. Current Processes: Looking at the process maps presented, what parts are accurate and what may need correction or clarification?
The process flow AS-IS does not capture the current position, the Authority Automated most of it's services; the EIA process included. the only bit that was left out is the issue of publication of projects for public comments which is a requirement by law. The proposed mobile GIS inspection is ok however, it may face challanges in instances where a joint/multi agency physical site inspection is required.
The consultation with relevant lead agencies is anchored in the law and not specifically for the acquisition of data but for review and expert fedback.
The Authority issues a number of permits and licenses aside from the EIA License and the process flows vary per permiting/licensing regime, will be keen to see how the other processes will be incorporated into this system.

### A2. Current Processes (Completeness): Are there services, steps, or activities that were not included in the process maps?
Most of the NEMA services have been fully automated, the process map however indicates some activities as manual.

Aside from the physical site verification exercises and the publication of projects for public comments, the other processes are automated.

### B1. Proposed Processes: What thoughts do you have about how the proposed future processes may work in practice?
The Authority recently acquired a new intergrated licensing system which automated most of its services. there is need to intergrate the ENVIS system with this proposed system since the process work flows are already defined in our current system.

Site verification exercises may at times require physical multi agency visits based on the nature and complexity of the project, tying site visits to mobile GIS inspection may be restrictive.

Lead agency consultation is a requirement by law and cannot be by passed without amendments to the law.

The process flow does not provide room for the proponent to respond to concerns/issues raised by the Authority prior to licensing.

### B2. Proposed Improvements: What comments do you have about the proposed quick wins and priority improvements?
As it is, the Authortiy is required to visit each site prior to licensing, the proposed mobile GIS inspection will assist in reducing the time and resources spent when undertaking site inspection, it however needs to be open for cases when a physical site verification is unavoidable.

### C1. Records Management: What feedback do you have about the records and document types identified as priorities?
There was no demonstration of the records management process flow including the document types.
Unable to give feedback.

### C2. Records Registry: What challenges related to records storage, classification, or registry work should be highlighted?
Full automation of the Authority's registry operations from document receipt to archiving and disposal.

### D1. Service Automation: What feedback do you have about the services selected for automation?
The demonstrated service work flow is already automated within the Authority, integration of the two systems would be helpful

### D2. Platform Integration: What considerations should be noted for integration with systems such as eCitizen, IPRS, or payment platforms?
KRA, eCitizen, Kentrade, BRS, IPRS, Ardhisasa are relevant to the Authority's processes

### E1. Policy and Regulations: What policy or legal issues should be considered for implementation?
-Lack of legal framewokrs for electronic evidence (in case of litigations).
Cross County regulatory mismatch

### E2. Institutional Arrangements: What comments do you have about the proposed governance structure, including the Digital Transformation Unit?
The Transformation unit is feasible as long as there is clear integration of processes and functions for each work flow

### F1. Organisational Capacity: What skills gaps, training needs, or readiness issues should be noted?
Noting the ever changing technology, there is need for continous digital literacy enhancement over time

### F2. Leadership and Ownership: What observations do you have about leadership support and institutional ownership?
The leadreship goodwill will enhance implementation
