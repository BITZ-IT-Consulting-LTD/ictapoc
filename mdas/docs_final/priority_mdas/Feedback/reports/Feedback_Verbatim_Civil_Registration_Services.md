Room 1: High Impact & Large Registries

### Participant Name (Optional)
Victor Rop

Civil Registration Services

### Which MDA presentation are you giving feedback on?
Civil Registration Services

### A1. Current Processes: Looking at the process maps presented, what parts are accurate and what may need correction or clarification?
- The AS-IS process flow is incorrect because the hierarchy is misaligned and the sequence is out of order. 
-  Merging the death and birth events has not produced an accurate flow for the two processes.
- The CRS offices are currently operating under two parallel processes: a fully manual workflow and a semi‑manual workflow using the CRVSS system. The captured process flow mixes elements from both approaches, resulting in an inaccurate and inconsistent representation of the actual operations.
-

### A2. Current Processes (Completeness): Are there services, steps, or activities that were not included in the process maps?
Services Missing 
- Re-registration processes (Adoption, Recognition, and  Legitimization)
- Registration of foreign births and deaths (Kenyan Citizens)
- Assumption of Death registration 
- Late Birth and Death Registration
-

### B1. Proposed Processes: What thoughts do you have about how the proposed future processes may work in practice?
- It is also important to acknowledge that CRS already has a fully developed end‑to‑end system known as CRVSS.
- CRS has already built a complete Birth and Death Notification App, and this should be adopted in place of the proposed Afya app. The Afya app is confined to medical facilities and captures insufficient information for CRS needs.

### B2. Proposed Improvements: What comments do you have about the proposed quick wins and priority improvements?
-  Birth and death registration processes are fundamentally different and should not be represented within a single process flow. It is important for the business analyst to acknowledge this and re‑evaluate the documentation to ensure the flows are clearly separated

### C1. Records Management: What feedback do you have about the records and document types identified as priorities?
CRS currently holds approximately 10 million records that remain in manual form. To ensure a smooth transition and prevent a backlog of manual records, a full digitization exercise is necessary, followed by a clear cutoff point to enable a complete shift to digital operations

### C2. Records Registry: What challenges related to records storage, classification, or registry work should be highlighted?
- Insufficient storage space  for the Birth and Death records has caused some records to be spoiled
- The digitization of the registries is crucial and urgent

### D1. Service Automation: What feedback do you have about the services selected for automation?
Birth and death registration services are core, high‑priority functions that require urgent digitalization. Although the two processes complement each other, they are distinct and must be documented separately to ensure accuracy and completeness

### D2. Platform Integration: What considerations should be noted for integration with systems such as eCitizen, IPRS, or payment platforms?
Yes, eCitizen and IPRS are already integrated with the CRVSS system. These integrations are essential for completing the end‑to‑end civil registration processes

### E1. Policy and Regulations: What policy or legal issues should be considered for implementation?
- Birth and Death Registration Act Cap 149
- The Registration of Persons Act
- The Children Act 2022
- Data Protection Act and Regulations
-  The Legitimacy Act
- Evidence Act

### E2. Institutional Arrangements: What comments do you have about the proposed governance structure, including the Digital Transformation Unit?
The proposal to have an established Digital Transformation unit is  a good idea

### F1. Organisational Capacity: What skills gaps, training needs, or readiness issues should be noted?
-  Training staff on the Civil Registration and Vital Statistics System
