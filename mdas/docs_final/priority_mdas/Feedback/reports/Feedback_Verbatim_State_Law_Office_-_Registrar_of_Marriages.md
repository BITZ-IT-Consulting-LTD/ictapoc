### A1. Current Processes: Looking at the process maps presented, what parts are accurate and what may need correction or clarification?
Executive Summary
The Office of the Attorney General & Department of Justice (through the Registrar of Marriages) is mandated to perform civil marriages; register all marriages; issue marriage certificates for all registered marriages; determine the rules governing customary marriages; determine objections of notices of intention to marry; appoint marriage officers at national and county levels; license Ministers of Faith to officiate marriages; register divorces; keep and maintain registers for all marriages and divorces; and undertake public education and awareness under the Marriage Act, Cap 150. The department plays a critical role in providing secure vital life-event records and family status documentation for citizens.

### A2. Current Processes (Completeness): Are there services, steps, or activities that were not included in the process maps?
MARRIAGE REGISTRATION WORK FLOW
1.	Application Submission and Review
a)	Client submits marriage application through the E-citizen Marriages portal.
b)	The application is queued as a backend task.
c)	Marriage Clerk retrieves applications from the queue on a first-come, first-served basis.
d)	The Marriage Clerk reviews the application and may:
•	Return it to the client for correction;
•	Approve the application; or
•	Reject the application.

2.	Notice Fee Payment & Public Notice
a)	Upon approval, the client is notified through a text to the provided mobile number to pay the prescribed Notice Fee via the e-Citizen portal.
b)	After successful payment, the system automatically forwards the application to the noticeboard.
c)	The client is notified via SMS that the notice has been posted to the public online noticeboard on the ecitizen marriages portal.
d)	The application is displayed on the public noticeboard for 21 days.

3.	Appointment Booking & Interview
a)	After the 21-day notice period lapses, the client is notified via sms to log in to E-citizen portal and book an interview appointment.
b)	On the appointment date, the parties appear before a gazetted Registrar with original documents.
c)	The interview is conducted to:
•	Determine the marital and mental capacity of the parties; and
•	Verify the original supporting documents.
d)	After the interview, the gazetted Registrar may:
•	Send the application back for correction;
•	Request for additional documents;
•	Reject the application; or
•	Approve the application and commission affidavits signed by the parties.





4.	Booking and Celebration
a)	Upon successful interview and approval, the parties are notified to pay the prescribed fee.
b)	Once payment is done a Registrar’s Certificate is auto-generated by the system.
c)	 The gazetted Registrar then books a marriage date for the parties on the system.
d)	On the scheduled marriage date, the parties with their witnesses appear at the Registrar’s office or designated venue for marriage solemnisation.
e)	The gazetted Registrar officiates the marriage and issues marriage certificates to the parties.
f)	A triplicate copy of the signed marriage certificate is scanned and uploaded into the system to create a database of marriages.


Registration of divorces
•	The court delivers certified copy divorce decree absolute to the Registrar of Marriages.
•	The Registrar registers the divorce in a divorce register.

### B1. Proposed Processes: What thoughts do you have about how the proposed future processes may work in practice?
1. Proposed processes to be aligned the workflow forwarded hereinbefore.
2. There is need for amendment of the Marriage Act and Rules to address the emerging issues and digitization processes.
3. Other services being offered by the Registrar should be included for digitization because marriage services are intertwined. These services include registration of marriage by notice (Christian and Hindu Marriages), registration of marriage by special licence, registration of existing and new customary marriages, issuance of certified copy of the marriage certificate, issuance of certificate of no impediment to marriage and licensing of ministers of faith. 
4. There is an urgent need for digitization  of all previous marriage records.
5. Integration with other registries for purposes of data sharing and validation/authentication.

### B2. Proposed Improvements: What comments do you have about the proposed quick wins and priority improvements?
1. Digitization of previous marriage records.
2. Complete the automation of the marriage and divorce registration process.
3. Training and capacity building to enhance employee skills and productivity.

### C1. Records Management: What feedback do you have about the records and document types identified as priorities?
Digitization  of all previous marriage records to be given priority.

### C2. Records Registry: What challenges related to records storage, classification, or registry work should be highlighted?
1. Inadequate storage space.
2. Most records are scattered all over the country since marriage function is delegated to Deputy County Commissioners (DCCs) and Ministers of Faith.
3. incorrect capturing of data on the marriage certificates especially by  licensed Ministers of Faith.
4. Failure to submit marriage returns by licensed Ministers of Faith as required by law.

### D1. Service Automation: What feedback do you have about the services selected for automation?
Other services being offered by the Registrar should be included for digitization because marriage services are intertwined. These services include registration of marriage by notice (Christian and Hindu Marriages), registration of marriage by special licence, registration of existing and new customary marriages, issuance of certified copy of the marriage certificate, issuance of certificate of no impediment to marriage and licensing of ministers of faith.

### D2. Platform Integration: What considerations should be noted for integration with systems such as eCitizen, IPRS, or payment platforms?
Integration of the marriage and divorce registration platform with other registries for purposes of data sharing and validation/authentication.

### E1. Policy and Regulations: What policy or legal issues should be considered for implementation?
