### A2. Current Processes (Completeness): Are there services, steps, or activities that were not included in the process maps?

### B1. Proposed Processes: What thoughts do you have about how the proposed future processes may work in practice?
National Police Service, Judiciary, Kenya Prison Service, PACS  and State Department for Correctional Services -HQ should have an  intergrated system.

### B2. Proposed Improvements: What comments do you have about the proposed quick wins and priority improvements?
FIRST WE NEED: we need to organise (appraise) the records in the registries before didgitization.
Update indexing and classification schemes in all organisation.
Training of staff.

### C1. Records Management: What feedback do you have about the records and document types identified as priorities?
1. Define standard data schemas and metadata (e.g., inmate ID, timestamps, facility codes)
2.Clearly define retention periods of each inmate record, Archiving policies and disposal procedures as it will reduce data overload or premature deletion of critical legal records.

### C2. Records Registry: What challenges related to records storage, classification, or registry work should be highlighted?
1.Lack of modern storage equipment.
2. No record centers.
3. Unupdated classification and indexing schemes.
4.Lack of appraising records.
5. Lack of skills and knowledge for staff handling records.

### D1. Service Automation: What feedback do you have about the services selected for automation?
1.Focus on High-Risk Manual Processes such as automation of: a)Sentence calculation & remission
 b)Release eligibility determination. 
This is critical because these areas currently have: High error rates and Legal implications if incorrect.

2.Digital Warrant Processing since it eliminates reliance on physical documents it Reduces: Forgery risks and delays in inmate admission.

### D2. Platform Integration: What considerations should be noted for integration with systems such as eCitizen, IPRS, or payment platforms?
1. Security & Data Protection
since:
-Inmate records include biometrics, criminal history, and health data
-Enforce Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
-Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for system users 

2. Interoperability & Standards
Systems must “talk to each other” seamlessly:
Judiciary → Prison System
Prison System → Probation
Identity verification via IPRS

### E1. Policy and Regulations: What policy or legal issues should be considered for implementation?
1. Public Archives and Documentation Service Act cap 19 and others.
2. Data Protection Act and others.

### E2. Institutional Arrangements: What comments do you have about the proposed governance structure, including the Digital Transformation Unit?
It should reflect centralized coordination clear accountability and structured digital oversight.

### F1. Organisational Capacity: What skills gaps, training needs, or readiness issues should be noted?
Information Technology Skills.

### F2. Leadership and Ownership: What observations do you have about leadership support and institutional ownership?
There are indications of support at the State Department level but the top management have not been sensitized.
