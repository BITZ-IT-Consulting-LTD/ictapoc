# ICTA Validation Workshop: Consolidated Stakeholder Feedback Report

## 1. Introduction
This document provides a consolidated overview of the feedback received from various Ministries, Departments, and Agencies (MDAs) during the ICTA Validation Workshops. The feedback was captured via the Kobo Toolbox submission system and reflects the requirements for refining the Business Process Architecture (BPA) for priority government services.

## 2. Executive Summary of Feedback
The consultations revealed a strong shift from basic automation requirements towards advanced digital enhancements. Key themes include the need for legal framework alignment (PFMA, Marriage Act), deep integration with national registries (IPRS, BRS), and the urgent requirement for large-scale digitization of historical records.

## 4. Key Global Trends & Priorities

### 4.1 Digitization vs. Enhancement
MDAs with existing systems (NEMA, CRS) are prioritizing "Intelligent" features such as **AI Rules Engines**, **GIS Geo-tagging**, and **Predictive Analytics** over simple digital data entry.

### 4.2 Legal & Regulatory Bottlenecks
Several MDAs cited the **PFMA Act** and the **Marriage Act** as critical dependencies. These acts currently mandate "physical signatures" or "manual vouchers," which must be addressed via policy reform to enable 100% digital Straight-Through Processing (STP).

### 4.3 The "Digital Identity" Anchor
Integration with **IPRS (Maisha Namba)** and **BRS** (Business Registration Service) is no longer seen as optional; it is the universal requirement for ensuring data trust across all G2C and G2B services.

### 4.4 Massive Record Backlogs
The success of the TO-BE "Maisha" ecosystem depends on the digitization of massive historical backlogs (e.g., the **10 Million records at CRS**). This requires a dedicated budget for back-file conversion alongside system deployment.
