# Organization of the Government of Kenya: Ministries and MDAs

Based on the most recent web search results for 2026, the primary document governing the organization of the Government of the Republic of Kenya is **Executive Order No. 1 of 2025** (issued in June 2025), which remains the operative structure for 2026, aligning the government with the FY 2025/26 budget and the "Bottom-Up Economic Transformation Agenda" (BETA). 

There is currently no Executive Order No. 1 of 2026 that overhauls this structure; however, regular updates and leadership appointments to various State Corporations (MDAs) are continuously published in the **Kenya Gazette** in 2026.

## Key Structural Changes and Realignments

The reorganization established **seven new State Departments** and realigned several ministries to improve service delivery:

### 1. New State Departments Established:
*   **National Government Co-ordination** (under the Office of the Prime Cabinet Secretary).
*   **Science, Research and Innovation** (under the Ministry of Education).
*   **Public Investments and Assets Management** (under the National Treasury).
*   **Special Programmes** (under the Ministry of Public Service).
*   **Aviation and Aerospace Development** (under the Ministry of Roads and Transport).
*   **Children’s Services** (under the Ministry of Gender and Culture).
*   **Justice, Human Rights and Constitutional Affairs** (under the Office of the Attorney-General).

### 2. Major Realignments and Changes:
*   **Executive Office of the President:** Absorbed the functions of the former State Department for Performance and Delivery Management, which was wound up.
*   **Office of the Prime Cabinet Secretary:** Retained its role in coordinating government legislative agendas and now oversees the State Department for National Government Co-ordination.
*   **Ministry of Public Service, Human Capital Development and Special Programmes:** Expanded to include the new Special Programmes department.
*   **Budgetary Alignment:** The National Treasury was directed to align the ministerial budgets for the FY 2025/26 to match these new mandates.

## Current Ministry & MDA Landscape (As of 2026)

The government remains organized into approximately **23 Ministries** and over **50 State Departments**. Key portfolios include:

*   **Interior & National Administration:** Focuses on internal security and the rollout of digital ID systems.
*   **Defence:** Oversees military welfare and regional security.
*   **The National Treasury & Economic Planning:** Manages public investment reforms and the transition to the 2026/27 fiscal cycle.
*   **Foreign & Diaspora Affairs:** Led by the Prime Cabinet Secretary; prioritizes "Economic and Commercial Diplomacy" and regional integration.
*   **Education:** Manages the implementation of the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) and the 2026 KJSEA (Kenya Junior School Education Assessment).
*   **Health:** Manages the transition to the Social Health Authority (SHA) and interoperable digital health systems.

## The Schedule of Functions

A critical component of Executive Order No. 1 of 2025 is the **Schedule of Functions**, which serves as the technical core defining the operational reality for the MDAs:

*   **Mandates:** Explicitly defines the specific legal and policy responsibilities of each Ministry and State Department.
*   **Institutions:** Provides a comprehensive list of State Corporations, Agencies, and Statutory Bodies assigned to each specific portfolio.
*   **Budgetary Alignment:** The National Treasury is directed to use this schedule as the basis for aligning ministerial budgets for the FY 2025/26, ensuring funding matches the newly assigned mandates.

## Purpose and Impact of the Reorganization

The strategic goals of this organizational structure include:
*   **Optimizing Resource Allocation:** Grouping related functions (e.g., Aviation and Aerospace) to reduce duplication of effort.
*   **Strengthening Oversight:** Particularly in areas like public investments and human rights.
*   **Centralizing Performance Tracking:** By moving delivery management directly to the Executive Office of the President, ensuring tighter oversight of flagship projects and the Bottom-Up Economic Transformation Agenda (BETA).

## Recent 2026 Administrative Updates

Administrative updates, primarily concerning leadership of MDAs, have been made in early 2026 through the **Kenya Gazette**:
*   **Appointments:** New chairpersons and board members have been appointed to various State Corporations, including the Kenya Deposit Insurance Corporation (KDIC), Kenya Film Classification Board (KFCB), and the Tana and Athi Rivers Development Authority (TARDA).
*   **Judiciary Designations:** Special Magistrates' Courts were designated at major border points to handle specific cases.

For exhaustive, granular details of each MDA's mandate and the Acts of Parliament they administer, the **Schedule of Functions** attached to Executive Order No. 1 of 2025 is the authoritative reference document.
