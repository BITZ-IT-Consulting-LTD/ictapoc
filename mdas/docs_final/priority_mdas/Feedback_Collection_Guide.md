# Stakeholder Feedback Guide: ICTA Validation Workshop

## Document Purpose
This guide defines the structured feedback required from Ministries, Departments, and Agencies (MDAs) during the ICTA Assessment Study Validation Workshop (March 16–18, 2026). The guide serves as a reference for:
*   Workshop facilitators
*   Rapporteurs
*   Participating MDAs

...to ensure feedback collected during the workshop is structured, actionable, and aligned with Phase 6: Validation and Finalisation of the assessment study. The feedback gathered will directly inform the finalisation of the assessment report, prioritisation frameworks, and procurement documentation for implementation.

## 1. Overall Objectives of Feedback Collection
As the project transitions from assessment to implementation planning, the workshop aims to collect stakeholder feedback to achieve the following objectives:

1. **Validate Accuracy**
   Confirm that the documented "As-Is" processes, records inventories, and infrastructure assessments accurately represent the current operational realities of the MDAs.
2. **Assess Feasibility**
   Evaluate whether the proposed "To-Be" processes, automation strategies, and digitisation roadmaps are feasible within existing institutional, technical, and financial constraints.
3. **Identify Gaps**
   Identify any missing workflows, regulatory considerations, compliance obligations, or operational constraints that were not captured during the assessment phase.
4. **Secure Stakeholder Endorsement**
   Obtain formal stakeholder support for the proposed frameworks, enabling the project to proceed to:
   *   Final report submission
   *   Preparation of procurement documentation (RFPs)
   *   Implementation planning.

## 2. Day 1: Plenary Presentations Feedback
*Focus: National Evidence Base, Frameworks, and Cross-Cutting Infrastructure.*

Day 1 presentations will provide a high-level overview of national findings, institutional readiness, and proposed digital transformation frameworks. Feedback from MDAs should focus on validating the strategic direction and policy implications of the project deliverables.

### Key Feedback Areas

**Draft Report & Key Findings**
Participants should review the national-level findings and provide feedback on:
*   Accuracy of infrastructure and digital maturity assessments
*   Relevance of the prioritisation framework
*   Alignment with national digital transformation goals

*Validation Questions*
*   Do the findings accurately reflect the digital readiness of MDAs?
*   Are any critical institutional or infrastructure constraints missing?
*   Are the recommended priorities appropriate?

**Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)**
Feedback should focus on the proposed PKI architecture and proof-of-concept implementation. Key considerations include:
*   Identity verification
*   Secure authentication
*   Integration with government services

*Validation Questions*
*   Does the PKI model align with the identity and authentication needs of MDAs?
*   What integration challenges are anticipated?
*   Are there existing authentication systems that must be considered?

**SME Compliance (KNADS, IPRS, ODPC, CAK)**
The proposed frameworks must comply with national regulatory standards. Feedback should assess the impact of the project on:
*   Data protection compliance (ODPC)
*   Records management and archival standards (KNADS)
*   Identity verification requirements (IPRS)
*   Communications regulation (CAK)

*Validation Questions*
*   Are there compliance risks that must be addressed before implementation?
*   What additional regulatory guidance is required?

## 3. Day 2: MDA Process Feedback (Breakout Sessions)
*Focus: MDA-specific workflows, records management, and e-service delivery.*

This session forms the core validation exercise, where MDAs will review both their current state ("As-Is") and proposed future state ("To-Be") processes. Observers and participating MDAs are expected to provide structured feedback across the following six pillars.

### A. As-Is Processes (Current State)
**Accuracy**
Confirm whether the documented processes accurately reflect operational realities.
*Validation Questions*
*   Are the current process flows accurate?
*   Are the roles and responsibilities correctly captured?

**Completeness**
Determine whether any services or operational steps were missed.
*Validation Questions*
*   Are there additional citizen-facing services not captured?
*   Are there undocumented manual steps currently in use?

### B. To-Be Processes (Future State)
**Feasibility**
Assess whether the proposed digital workflows are implementable.
*Validation Questions*
*   Are the proposed changes realistic given the MDA's current capacity?
*   What operational constraints might affect implementation?

**Strategic Alignment**
Ensure proposed improvements align with institutional goals.
*Validation Questions*
*   Do the recommended "quick wins" address the most critical service delivery gaps?
*   Do the proposed improvements support the MDA’s strategic objectives?

### C. Records Management & Digitisation
**Prioritisation**
Review the prioritisation of records for digitisation.
*Validation Questions*
*   Are the identified record categories correct?
*   Are there sensitive records that require special handling?

**Registry Challenges**
Evaluate challenges related to physical records management.
*Validation Questions*
*   Are there major storage or classification challenges not captured?
*   Are current registry practices adequately reflected?

### D. Automation (e-Service)
**Workflow Prioritisation**
Evaluate the services selected for automation.
*Validation Questions*
*   Will automating these services significantly improve citizen access?
*   Are higher-impact services missing?

**Integration**
Assess dependencies on shared government platforms.
*Validation Questions*
*   Are integrations with platforms such as eCitizen, IPRS, or national payment gateways required?
*   Are there additional systems that must be integrated?

### E. Governance
**Policy & Compliance**
Identify legal and regulatory constraints.
*Validation Questions*
*   Are there legislative barriers to digitisation or automation?
*   Are there ODPC compliance issues to consider?

**Institutional Framework**
Evaluate proposed governance structures.
*Validation Questions*
*   Is the proposal to establish Digital Transformation Units within MDAs feasible?
*   What additional governance mechanisms are needed?

### F. Capacity Building & Change Management
**Organisational Readiness**
Assess workforce readiness.
*Validation Questions*
*   What digital skills gaps currently exist?
*   What training programs are required?

**Leadership Support**
Evaluate institutional commitment.
*Validation Questions*
*   Is there executive sponsorship within the MDA?
*   What leadership structures are required to sustain transformation?

## 4. Day 3: Synthesis & Endorsement Feedback
*Focus: Consolidation of findings and agreement on the path forward.*

The final day will present consolidated feedback from the breakout sessions and seek formal validation of the project outputs.

### Key Feedback Areas

**Synthesis Accuracy**
Participants should confirm that the consolidated findings reflect the workshop discussions.
*Validation Questions*
*   Does the summary accurately reflect the discussions held during the breakout sessions?
*   Are there key issues missing from the synthesis?

**Endorsement Status**
MDAs will be asked to confirm whether they support the proposed outputs, including:
*   Process Maps
*   Digitisation Prioritisation Framework
*   Automation Roadmaps
*   Governance Models

**Conditional Endorsement**
If endorsement is provided with conditions, participants should clearly specify:
*   Outstanding issues
*   Required revisions
*   Additional consultations needed before implementation.
